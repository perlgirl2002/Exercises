You're testing advertising emails for a bathing suit company and you test one version of the email in February and the other in May.
People are typically not shopping for bathing suits in February, but would have a higher click through rate in May when it is closer to summer.


You open a clinic to treat anxiety and find that the people who visit show a higher rate of anxiety than the general population.
The population of people visiting the clinic would have a higher tendency to visit an anxiety clinic IF they had anxiety to begin with. 

You launch a new ad billboard based campaign and see an increase in website visits in the first week.
You would expect an increase in the first week if the campaign was successful in the billboard.  But you can't be sure that it is related to the billboard.  

You launch a loyalty program but see no change in visits in the first week.
Too many unobserved variables here.  There could be a lot of different reasons why no change in visits in the first week.  The earth was destroyed.  Networks collapsed.  People never saw the invitation to the loyalty program.
